The Royalty Model

Flexera bundles Liquit's Product (Package Feed + Vendor Patch data) with Flexera Software to create the Integrated Product it sells to end customers. Liquit earns a percentage of Flexera's direct customer revenue under a tiered table with a per-customer minimum floor. Both new sales and renewals are paid under the same tier table — there is no rate distinction between net-new and renewal revenue.

Tier-threshold interpretation

The contract is silent on whether tier thresholds reset annually or accumulate from inception. Flexera operationally treats them as cumulative since March 22, 2019 — the implied rate steps down at exactly the cumulative $1M, $2M, $4M, and $6M crossings. The currently active tier is highlighted in cyan below.

Other terms worth flagging: $50,000 annual minimum (credits clawed back if Flexera underbills); Net-60 days from valid invoice receipt; no royalty on training, services, or non-Product fees (Section 8.4); 3-year post-termination support window.

Customer-level churn of 79.1% on mature accounts is materially out of band

Of 153 customers whose latest subscription has had time to renew, 121 have not. Best-in-class B2B SaaS partnerships typically run 5–15% gross logo churn. A 79% non-renewal rate suggests one of three things: (1) genuine product churn, (2) Flexera renewing customers without flagging them as Liquit-royalty-bearing, or (3) Flexera migrating customers to a Flexera-native substitute that doesn't bundle Liquit (which would carry zero royalty per Section 8.4). Renewal Economics Model — Should-Be vs. Actual
Adjustable assumptions · Customer cohorts · Year-by-year reconciliation
How this model works

For each year, we count new customers Flexera actually acquired (from royalty reports — not projected) and add modeled renewals from prior cohorts. Each customer added in year Y is assumed to renew at the slider's Renewal Rate in subsequent years, valued at their original first-deal price (no annual uplift). The Silent Renewal slider models customers we have classified as churned but who may have renewed at Flexera unbilled to Liquit.

The model gives the finance team an honest "what should this look like" baseline to compare against what was actually paid — and a way to translate slider assumptions directly into a dollar gap.

Model simplifications & caveats

1. Flat pricing — each renewal is valued at the cohort's average first-deal price (no annual uplift). In reality Flexera typically increases prices 3–7%/year, so the gap is likely understated.

2. New customers from history only — we don't project future new acquisitions. The gap shown is for the existing book only.

3. 2026 is partial — Q1 only, so 2026 figures represent ~25% of a full year and should not be annualized directly.

4. Tier rates are cumulative — should-be royalty applies the tier rate that would actually be in effect at that cumulative revenue level, which is typically lower than the actual rate Flexera paid in that period.

Source PDF shows line-item subtotal of $106,480.25 minus a credit memo of $77,435.52 ("using 40% instead of 35% past the 1M Revenue mark") plus a missed-minimums adjustment of $7,021.13 = final cash payment of $36,065.84. Workbook stores the line-item sum, not the post-credit total.
Q4 2019 — 5 lines have inconsistent EUR/USD treatment
For 5 of 15 lines (Prianto, Sigma, NBConsult, Prof Dev, Softline), the Amount column appears to be EUR but the Payment Due is USD — implied rate is 44.1% instead of 40%. Total impact on all-time revenue: ~$4,522 (0.07%). All royalty figures are correct.
Q3 2025 — three negative payment-due adjustments
Three lines (Emerging Market Tech, Waterschap, ABB) show -$175.98, -$208.11, -$494.37 as payment due. These are second-pass clawbacks when cumulative inception-to-date revenue crossed $6M Tier 5 boundary mid-quarter. Net Q3-2025 royalty of $71,622.95 matches PDF.

Source PDF for Harley-Davidson line shows sub-end as raw Excel serial "46007" (= Dec 16, 2025). Workbook stores correctly as 2025-12-16. Cosmetic only.

Renewal Definition

For each unique normalized customer, find the latest subscription end date across all their orders. Then:

  • Active: latest sub_end is more than 180 days in the future
  • In Renewal Window: latest sub_end is within ±180 days of today
  • Churned: latest sub_end is more than 180 days in the past AND no further activity
  • No End Date: customer's records lack sub_end fields (1 customer, dropped from analysis)

The 180-day threshold is judgmental. A customer with a sub_end exactly 6 months ago might still renew late; a customer in the window today might be already gone. The threshold draws a defensible line for finance reporting but should not be treated as binary truth at the customer level.

What This Dashboard Does NOT Do

  • Verify per-customer minimum fee compliance for all 496 line items
  • Conclude whether the cumulative-since-inception tier interpretation is contractually correct (the Agreement is silent)
  • Make any legal claim about money owed by Flexera. Confirmation requires the audit conversation
  • Distinguish between Liquit Product and any Flexera-native substitute that may have replaced it for a given customer
